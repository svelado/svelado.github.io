The Problem

A huge problem in the marine industry is biofouling The accumulation of biofoulers on hulls can increase both the hydrodynamic volume of a vessel and the hydrodynamic friction, leading to increased drag of up to 60%,The drag increase has been seen to decrease speeds by up to 10%, which can require up to a 40% increase in fuel to compensate.With fuel typically comprising up to half of marine transport costs, antifouling methods are estimated to save the shipping industry around $60 billion per year. Increased fuel use due to biofouling contributes to adverse environmental effects and is predicted to increase emissions of carbon dioxide and sulfur dioxide between 38 and 72% by 2020. (From Wikipedia)

The Robot

We present our solution to the problem, the CERLAB Underwater robot. For this development stage, It's purpose is only to examine ship hulls for biofouling. This means our robot is going to be used with a camera. It has two phases of motion, the 3D motion it will be using while it is underwater and the planar 2D motion which is used when the robot attached itself to the ship hull, and depends only the omni wheels. Omni wheels mean we have control of our orientation at all times, which is important because we only have one camera. This also gives us the ability to move in any direction, regardless of our current orientation.

Planner

I programmed in C++, a multigoal weighted informed A* search planner. I chose this for a few goals. First, I wanted to have mutliple goals because during inspection there are always multiple areas to examine. Then, I wanted to make sure it was an informed search to improve it speed, since we want to minimize the amount of water damage. We use the euclidean distance as the heuristic. Finally, I added an additional weight to bias the planner towards unexplored areas. It is evident that that will lead to suboptimal paths, but since we want to be able to gather as much data from each dive, it's a good idea to go through unseen paths in case something we didn't know about happens to the hull, or we discover something interesting. I then used that planner and interfaced it with ROS, using my own custom messages and Rviz to visualize it.

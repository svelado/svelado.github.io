Bio

My name is Sidney Miguel Velado, but I often go by Mickey. I am currently studying Mechanical Engineering and Robotics at Carnegie Mellon. I hope someday I can combine my studys to push the boundaries of what robots can do. In my free time I love to watch European football and am an avid supporter of Arsenal FC. I also enjoy playing video games, with God Of War being one of my favorite game series.

Despite currently living in the United States, it's wasn't always my home. I was born in Guatemala, and lived in Peru, Bolivia, and finally settled in Costa Rica. I had the privilege to enjoy growing up in an amazing tropical climate full of adventures. It made me love the outdoors and drove my passion for football, as well as foster my sense of Latino culture and pride, which shines in everything I do.

Besides my current academics at CMU, I also have served in multiple student organizations. I am active in my fraternity Phi Delta Theta where I served as Social Chair as well as the IM Chair. I also serve in the Spanish and Latino Student Association as the Internal Outreach Chair and as a general member of the Society of Hispanic Professional Engineers.

Experience

Staples - Robotics Engineer Intern

This summer I had the pleasure of being an intern for one of the most iconic companies in the United States. Fresh off a rebrand, its been an exciting time to work at Staples. Staples is evolving in every facet possible, planning for the future to bring itself into our new technology era. One of these evolutions, is relying less on outside companies for their technological needs, and instead developing that technology in house. In the case of robotics, there are multiple supply chain operations and automation tasks that the company want to tackle with robotics. It is an ambitious task to establish a robotics department with no prior knowledge or expertise in that field; but a task that Staples is up for. Given my previous experience, I was brought on to help with this project as a Robotics Engineer Intern. I was a part of a small team of 7 people; where we focused building certain products, as well as developing core technologies that can be used across multiple different projects. I have not worked at a startup, but I imagine it is similar to this. Week in and week out we had to preform to show key stakeholders that we had what it took to bring the Robotics Department to life. Despite being such a small team it was impressive how we managed to cover most areas of expertise. I was the expert when it came to controls and hardware design. Many of my projects focused on developing core technologies for this, and I used my design skills to build different robots, work with different test platforms, as well as multiple mechatronics systems to test different technologies. I had a lot of responsiblity, which included me roadmapping my main project with milestones and deliverables, as well as presenting my work to executives in the company. I am grateful to Staples for giving me the opportunity to apply all the skills I learned at Carnegie Mellon, and giving me an environment where I was constantly challenged to do my best. I will always keep everything I learned and my amazing memories of my summer in Boston and Staples close to me as I seek new challenges!

Robotics Institute: Biorobotics Laboratory

The summer of after my junior year, I began working in the Robotics Institute Biorobotics Laboratory, ran by Professor Howie Choset. I had the privilege to continue the project started by master student Daniel Piedrahita, under the guidance of a PhD candidate Steve Crews. The project called the Flyped, was a bipdeal robot with a fly wheel which it uses to maintain balance and accomplish complex movements. I used my C++ and Python skills to create the ROS package to implement our controller. I also used my Solidworks skills to make multiple different components for our robot, and even redesigned the entire robot from scratch. I am excited to be a part of the group of students working on some cutting edge research and look forward to publishing something soon!

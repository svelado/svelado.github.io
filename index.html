Bio

My name is Sidney Miguel Velado, but I often go by Mickey. I am currently a Mechanical Engineering and Robotics double major at Carnegie Mellon. I hope someday I can combine my degreeds to push the boundaries of what robots can do. I'm really interested in learning more about motion/path planning and computer vision. In my free time I love to watch European football and am an avid supporter of the Arsenal. I also enjoy playing video games, with God Of War being the latest game I binged.

Despite currently living in the United States, it's wasn't always my home. I was born in Guatemala, and lived in Peru, Bolivia, and finally settled in Costa Rica. I had the privilege to enjoy growing up in an amazing tropical climate full of adventures. It made me love the outdoors and drove my passion for football, as well as foster my sense of Latino culture and pride, which shines in everything I do.

Besides my current academics at CMU, I also have served in multiple student organizations. I am active in my fraternity Phi Delta Theta where I served as Social Chair as well as the IM Chair. I also serve in the Spanish and Latino Student Association as the Internal Outreach Chair and as a general member of the Society of Hispanic Professional Engineers.

Robotics Institute: Biorobotics Laboratory

Over my junior year summer, I began working in the Robotics Institute Biorobotics Laboratory, ran by Professor Howie Choset. I had the privilidge to continue the project started by a graduate student Daniel Piedrahita, under the guidance of a PhD candidate Steve Crews. The project called the Flyped, was a bipdeal robot with a fly wheel that it uses for movement and maintian balance. At first, I started with no ROS knowledge and used my skills in Solidworks to provide a lot of the CAD and make hardware upgrades. Additionally, I have used my machinery skills to bring the concepts to life and produce the parts I model. Since then, I picked up a lot of very important skills. I learned how to work in a Linux environment, I also became experienced in ROS and sharpened my C++ and Python skills. I am currently working on an overall hardware redesign, Since the first robot design has a lot of inconsistencies and doesn't work the way we need it to. I am excited to be a part of the group of students working on some cutting edge research and look forward to publishing something soon!
